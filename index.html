What are world heritage sites?
World Heritage Sites are natural or cultural landmarks recognized by UNESCO for their "Outstanding Universal Value". These sites are of immense importance to humanity and are protected for future generations.
As of July 2024, there are a total of 1,223 World Heritage Sites located across 168 countries. These sites are categorized as cultural (952), natural (231), or mixed (40) properties.
