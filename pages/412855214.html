World Heritage Sites
Asia
Great wall of China
Stretching over 20,000 kilometers, the Great Wall was built over centuries to protect Chinese states from invasions. It stands as a monumental feat of ancient engineering and military strategy.
China
Contrary to myth, the Great Wall is not visible from space with the naked eye.
Taj Mahal
Agra, India
The Taj Mahal is a white marble mausoleum built by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. It is celebrated for its exquisite symmetry and romantic backstory.
The color of the Taj Mahal appears to change depending on the time of day and the light.
Angkor Wat
Angkor was the capital of the Khmer Empire and contains the massive temple complex of Angkor Wat, the largest religious structure in the world. The site blends Hindu and Buddhist architecture and cosmology.
China
Angkor Wat appears on Cambodia’s national flag — one of the few national flags to feature a building.
Historic area of Istanbul
Istanbul, Turkey
Spanning Europe and Asia, Istanbul was once Byzantium and Constantinople, capital to both the Byzantine and Ottoman Empires. The city’s historic area showcases iconic landmarks like Hagia Sophia and the Blue Mosque.
Istanbul is the only city in the world that sits on two continents.
