Acropolis of Athens
Greece
The Acropolis is a symbol of classical Greek civilization and democracy, housing iconic structures like the Parthenon. It represents ancient architectural brilliance and philosophical ideals that shaped Western culture.
The Parthenon was originally painted in bright colors, not the white marble look we associate it with today.
Stonehenge, Avebury and Associated Sites
Wiltshire, England, United Kingdom
Stonehenge is a prehistoric stone circle whose exact purpose remains a mystery, though it's believed to have religious or astronomical significance. It stands as one of the most famous and enigmatic monuments in the world.
Some of the stones at Stonehenge were transported from over 150 miles away in Wales.

Palace and Park of Versailles
Versailles, France
The Palace of Versailles epitomizes the grandeur and excess of French absolute monarchy, especially under Louis XIV. Its vast gardens, ornate halls, and intricate fountains make it a masterpiece of 17th-century European art.
The famous Hall of Mirrors contains 357 mirrors and was the site where the Treaty of Versailles was signed in 1919.
